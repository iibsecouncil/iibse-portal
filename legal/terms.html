1. About IIBSE Council

IIBSE Council is an autonomous, non-profit educational council established under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, with the objective of promoting education, skills, and value-based learning.

The Council functions with a social vision to support inclusive education, skill development, and holistic learner growth, particularly for underserved and rural communities.

2. Constitutional & Legal Framework

IIBSE Council derives its educational philosophy from the Constitution of India, including but not limited to:

  • Article 19(1)(g) – Right to carry on educational activities
  • Article 21A – Right to Education
  • Article 29 & 30 – Cultural and institutional rights
  • Article 45 – Early childhood care and education
  • Article 51A – Promotion of scientific temper, ethics, and harmony

3. Educational Philosophy & Alignment

IIBSE Council promotes a holistic education model integrating academics, skill-based education, vocational exposure, ethics, and values, aligned with:

  • National Education Policy (NEP) 2020
  • National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF)

4. Nature & Registration

IIBSE Council functions as a private, autonomous, self-regulated, non-profit educational body. It is:

  • Registered under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), Government of India
  • Registered with NITI Aayog, Government of India

IIBSE Council operates independently and does not claim statutory or government board status.

5. Admissions Policy

Admissions under IIBSE-affiliated institutions are governed by institutional eligibility criteria, academic norms, and applicable guidelines. Direct admission services, where offered, are facilitative in nature.

Submission of applications does not guarantee admission and is subject to verification, eligibility, and institutional approval.

6. Examination & Certification

Examinations, assessments, and certifications supported by IIBSE Council are conducted under approved academic frameworks and evaluation systems.

Certificates issued reflect academic or skill-based completion as per applicable guidelines and do not imply equivalence with statutory government examinations unless explicitly stated.

7. Fees & Service Policy

Fees charged by IIBSE Council are applied exclusively toward academic development, administrative processes, training, certification, examination support, and educational services.

All fees once paid are non-refundable, including registration, affiliation, examination, certification, training, or service charges.
